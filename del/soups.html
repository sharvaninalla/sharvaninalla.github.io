CORN SOUP

INGREDIENTS
1 1/4 cup (3/4 cup + 1/2 cup) Sweet Corn Kernels, boiled (approx. 2 small corn cobs)
1/2 tablespoon Corn flour (cornstarch)
1/4 cup chopped Carrot
1/4 cup chopped Spring Onion (green onion)
1/4 teaspoon Black Pepper Powder
1 teaspoon Butter or Oil
2 cups Water or Vegetable Stock
Salt to taste

Note: If spring onion (green onion) is not available, use the same amount of normal onion instead of it in this recipe.

INSTRUCTIONS
1. Add sweet corn cobs, 2 cups water and salt in a pressure cooker and cook them for 2-3 whistles over medium flame. Turn off the flame and let the pressure releases naturally. Open the lid, drain the water and let them cool for few minutes. Remove the corn kernels using a knife. Dissolve 1/2 tablespoon corn flour (corn starch) in 1/4 cup water and keep aside.
2. Add 3/4 cup boiled sweet corn kernels along with 1/4 cup water in the small jar of a blender or a mixer grinder.
3. Grind it to a smooth puree.
4. Heat 1-teaspoon butter in a large pan over medium flame. Add chopped carrot and spring onions. Stir and cook for a minute.
5. Add pureed corn and mix well. Stir and cook for 2-minutes.
6. Add remaining (1/2 cup) boiled sweet corn kernels.
7. Add 2-cups vegetable stock (or water) and black pepper powder.
8. Add salt to taste and mix well. Let it cook for 3-4 minutes. Add dissolved corn flour mixture. Stir continuously for a minute to mix well.
9. Cook until soup turns little thick, for around 4-5 minutes. Turn off the flame. Sweet Corn Soup is ready to serve.

VEG MANCHOW SOUP


INGREDIENTS
For fried noodles:
4 cup water
1 tsp salt
1 tsp oil
1 pack hakka noodles
1 tsp cornflour
oil (for frying)

For soup:
2 tbsp oil
1 inch ginger (finely chopped)
2 clove garlic (finely chopped)
2 chilli (finely chopped)
1/2 onion (finely chopped)
1/2 carrot (finely chopped)
3 tbsp cabbage (finely chopped)
1/2 capsicum (finely chopped)
5 beans (finely chopped)
2 tbsp coriander stem (chopped)
4 cup water
3/4 tsp salt
2 tbsp soy sauce
2 tbsp vinegar
1/2 tsp pepper powder
1 tsp chilli sauce
1 tsp cornflour
1/4 cup water (for slurry)
2 tbsp coriander (finely chopped)

INSTRUCTIONS
Fried noodles preparation:
1. Firstly, in a large vessel take 4 cup water, 1 tsp salt and 1 tsp oil, once the water comes to a boil, break in 1 pack hakka noodles.
2. Boil for 5 minutes or refer package instructions to know the cooking time.
3. Cook until the noodles turn al dente without overcooking and drain off the noodles and rinse with cold water to stop the cooking process.
4. Now add 1 tsp cornflour and mix gently.
5. Deep fry in hot oil by spreading noodles uniformly.Stir occasionally, keeping the flame on medium.Flip over and fry both sides until the noodles turn crisp.
6. Finally, drain off the crispy noodles and keep aside.
Manchow soup preparation:
1. Firstly, in a large kadai heat 2 tbsp oil, saute 1 inch ginger, 2 clove garlic and 2 chilli, also, saute 1/2 onion until it shrinks slightly.
2. Now add 1/2 carrot, 3 tbsp cabbage, 1/2 capsicum, 5 beans and 2 tbsp coriander stem.
3. Stir fry for a minute without overcooking them,now pour 4 cup water and add 3/4 tsp salt. mix well and boil until all the flavours are well absorbed.
4. Now add 2 tbsp soy sauce, 2 tbsp vinegar, 1/2 tsp pepper powder and 1 tsp chilli sauce. mix well,in a small bowl take 1 tsp cornflour and mix with 1/4 cup water.
5. Pour the cornflour slurry and stir well.
6. Stir and boil until the soup thickens slightly and turns glossy.
7. Now add 2 tbsp coriander and give a good mix.
8. Finally, enjoy veg manchow soup recipe topped with fried noodles.

HOT AND SOUR SOUP

INGREDIENTS
2 teaspoons Oil
1/4 cup finely chopped Onion
1 teaspoon finely chopped Ginger
1 teaspoon finely chopped Garlic
1 tablespoon finely chopped Celery, optional
1/4 cup finely chopped Cabbage
2-3 tablespoons finely chopped Green French Beans
2-3 tablespoons finely chopped Carrot
2-3 tablespoons finely chopped Capsicum (Green Pepper)
2 cups + 1/4 cup Vegetable Stock or Water
5 teaspoons Cornflour (corn starch)
1 tablespoon Soy Sauce
1/2 tablespoon Chilli Sauce
1/4 teaspoon Black Pepper Powder
1/2 teaspoon Vinegar, optional
Salt to taste

INSTRUTIONS
1. Finely chop all the vegetables(cabbage, green beans, carrot, capsicum) listed in the ingredients table. Dissolve 5 teaspoons cornflour(corn starch) in 1/4 cup water.
2. Heat 2 teaspoons oil in a deep pot or a kadai over medium flame. Add 1/4 cup finely chopped onion and sauté for 2-3 minutes or until it turns translucent.
3. Add 1 teaspoon finely chopped ginger, 1 teaspoon finely chopped garlic and 1 tablespoon finely chopped celery.
4. Sauté for 30 seconds.
5. Add finely chopped vegetables(1/4 cup cabbage, 2-3 tablespoons green beans, 2-3 tablespoons carrot, 2-3 tablespoons capsicum).
6. Sauté for 2-minutes.
7. Add 2 cups water or vegetable stock.
8. Add salt to taste (if you are using the vegetable stock then be careful while adding the salt).
9. Add 1 tablespoon soy sauce.
10. Add 1/2 tablespoon chilli sauce and mix well.
11. Add dissolved cornflour-water mixture.
12. Mix well. Add 1/4 teaspoon black pepper powder. Let the soup simmer for 5-6 minutes or until it thickens slightly.
13.  Add 1/2 teaspoon vinegar and mix well.
14. Turn off the flame. Hot and Sour Soup is ready for serving. Garnish it with green onions and serve.

TOMATO SOUP

INGREDIENTS
4 large Tomatoes
1 1/2 tablespoons chopped Beetroot (or a small piece), optional
3-4 cloves of Garlic, chopped
7-8 Whole Black Peppercorns
A small piece of Bay Leaf, optional
1/2 tablespoon Butter
1 teaspoon Maida (all purpose flour)
1 teaspoon Sugar 
1 1/2 cups Water
Salt to taste

INSTRUCTIONS
1. Dice tomatoes into quarters and beetroot into small pieces.
2. Add tomatoes, beetroot, garlic, black peppercorns and bay leaf in a medium saucepan or a 2-3 liter capacity pressure cooker. Add 1-cup water and salt and cook covered until beetroot and tomatoes turn soft. It will take around 8-10 minutes over medium flame. If you are using a pressure cooker, pressure-cook them over medium flame for 2 whistles.
3. When they turn soft, turn off the flame. Remove the lid and let the mixture cool at room temperature for a few minutes.
4. Discard the bay leaf and blend the mixture using a hand blender (stick blender) or in the jar of a mixer grinder until smooth puree. Be careful while making the puree, as the mixture is hot.
5. Strain prepared puree through a sieve or a strainer in a bowl. Discard the residue. Do not use a very fine strainer, as we need tomato pulp in the soup.
6. Heat 1/2 tablespoon butter in the same saucepan over medium flame. Add 1-teaspoon maida.
7. Stir continuously and cook for a minute.
8. Add strained tomato puree little by little and stir continuously to avoid lumps formations. Add 1/2 cup water, 1-teaspoon sugar and mix well.
9. Bring it to boil over high flame. When it comes to rolling boil, reduce flame to medium and cook for 4-5 minutes. Taste for salt and pepper at this stage and add more if required. Turn off the flame. Tomato Soup is ready. Garnish with black pepper powder and coriander leaves and serve with bread croutons.

CREAM OF BROCCOLI SOUP

INGREDIENTS
200 gm broccoli-chopped fine
2 Tbsp butter/oil
2 Tbsp sifted flour
1/4 tsp powdered black pepper
3/4 cup milk4 cups stock or water
1/2 tsp celery salt
2 tsp saltCream for garnishing

INSTRUCTIONS
1. In a heavy saucepan heat the butter or oil.
2. When the butter has melted or the oil is steaming, add half the broccoli.
3. Turn around a few times, then lower the heat and continue sautéing for about half a minute.
4. Add the flour and turn around till the mixture leaves the sides of the pan.
5. Remove from heat, and add the stock very gradually, stirring all the time (thus avoiding lumps).
6. Cool, and blend in a blender.
7. Place the pan back over the heat, and add the rest of the broccoli, milk, black pepper and celery salt.
8. Cook over high heat, stirring a few times, till it comes to a boil and then simmer over low heat for at least 10 minutes.
9. Serve hot garnished with cream.

CHICKEN SOUP

INGREDIENTS:
250 gms chicken
1/4 tsp turmeric powder
2 cups water
2 nos garlic
To grind to a paste:
10 nos small onion
1/4 cup coriander leaves tightly packed
1 tsp cumin seeds
1 tsp pepper corns
To temper:
2 tsp oil
1 no cardamom
1/2 tsp cumin seeds
1 small sized big onion finely chopped

INSTRUCTIONS:
1. First grind the ingredients listed under 'to grind to a paste' with little water to a semi fine paste. Set aside.
2. In a pressure cooker heat oil – add cardamom and jeera let it splutter.
3. Then add onions and curry leaves saute till it is slightly browned.
4. Then add washed and cleaned chicken pieces, add turmeric powder. 5. Saute for 2mins then add the paste.
6. Saute well till raw smell leaves say for 3-5mins.
7. Then add water, required salt and pressure cook for 5 whistles. Once pressure releases, open and serve hot.
8. Serve Chicken Soup hot with any bread. We had it with masala bread.
